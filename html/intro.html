Introduction about Banksy’s Style
Active since the 1990s, Banksy's satirical street art and subversive epigrams combine dark humour with graffiti executed in a distinctive stenciling technique. Banksy devised a series of intricate stencils to minimise time and overlapping of the colour. His works of political and social commentary have appeared on streets, walls, and bridges throughout the world.
His artwork can be rebellious and is known for delivering political messages. Banksy's stencils feature striking and humorous images occasionally combined with slogans. The message is usually anti-war, anti-capitalist or anti-establishment. Subjects often include rats, apes, policemen, soldiers, children, and the elderly.
Banksy's art works used black in abundance, and spot colors on occasion. Black silhouette features strongly, and as much as possible there are no words unless words are absolutely required. Most of Banksy's art works are made by a handful of words and a single can of spray paint.
